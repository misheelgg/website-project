2025 Ars Electronica Festival

The motto of this year’s Ars Electronica Festival PANIC yes / no questions our social and technological developments. Should we be panicking? Experience outstanding Art & Science projects from JKU at the festival.

Because we are a place where science is created by people for people. Here, knowledge is not only shared but actively shaped - in a dialogue between disciplines, between science, art, and society, between theory and practice.

In a total of seven Art & Science projects, supported among others by the Linz Institute of Technology (LIT) at JKU, as well as further installations in cooperation with the Circus of Knowledge and the University of Art and Design Linz, JKU researchers and artists present unique interactive installations that invite reflection, participation, and discussion.

Visit the JKU projects in the POSTCITY free of charge.

JKU Projects at the Festival

In times when the world seems to be increasingly falling apart, the question of what will continue to hold it together at its core becomes ever more significant. For centuries, it is precisely these considerations that have driven science and art to extraordinary achievements. Only those willing to delve deeply into the foundations can create truly groundbreaking innovations.

That is why the Linz Institute of Technology (LIT) at JKU is once again presenting seven selected Art & Science projects this year, where research, artistic communication, and innovation intersect. At the Ars Electronica Festival, this interdisciplinary work of JKU - as well as further Art & Science initiatives - will be made accessible to a broad audience.
